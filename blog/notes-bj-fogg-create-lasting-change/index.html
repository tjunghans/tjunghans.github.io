Notes on BJ Fogg: Create Lasting Change

Notes from the interview with Shane Parish on the Farnam Street Knowledge Project Podcast.

Information alone does not reliably lead to sustained behavior change. For example, knowing that smoking is unhealthy does not keep many people from smoking. The same goes exercise and nutrition.

Seeing data won't necessarily change somebody's behavior in the long term.

Information about why you should do something in a field we already understand well, like nutrition, sleep or stress reduction, more information about why you should reduce your stress seems not to lead to lasting change.

Information about here's the behavior to do and here's how you do it, then that is a great approach.

Specific information about what behaviour and making it easier for people to do help forming new habits.

behavior happens when three things come together at the same moment, motivation, ability and prompt.

information alone does not change behavior.

1) Someone that wants to eat healthier needs to be trained or skilled up to cook healthier. This is changing the person and making them more capable. Many people don’t like changing to learning something new.

2) redesign environment. Add tools and resources. You’re changing the environment and not the person. This is the context the make the behaviour easier to do.

3) Bucket 3 of 3. Scale the behaviour back. So you take whatever action that is, such as, oh, I want to work out an hour a day and you scale it back to, well, maybe I'm just going to work out for four minutes. Make the action tinier.

3 buckets: You can change the person to make it easier to do it by scaling them up. This is the person model; skill up the person, change the environment or scale the behavior back.

We can change our environment by changing our perception of it. Example: studying a map about a new location you are in.

If there's no motivation, you will not do the behavior by definition

There are three core motivators:

  1. One is pleasure and pain. Core motivator for everyone
  2. anticipation. It's hope and fear. And it's the idea that if I do, X, Y will happen and if Y is a good thing, like, oh, if I take vitamin C, I won't get sick. That's hope. Whereas fear is, oh, if I touch this elevator button and get a virus, I'll get sick.
  3. third core motivator is a social one, which has to do with wanting to belong or the fear of being rejected.

what motivates human beings. pleasure, pain, hope and fear and social acceptance and social rejection.

Behaviour model behavior happens when these three things come together at the same moment, motivation, ability and prompt.

If motivation is low for any given behavior, then the ability must be high. In other words, it has to be easy to do. Otherwise, you won't do it.

If motivation is high you can do easy and hard things.

The feeling of success is what wires in a habbit and keeps people going.
